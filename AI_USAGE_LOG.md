

## Entry 1 — Understanding Family Challenges

**Date:** 14 July 2026  
**AI Tool Used:** ChatGPT

### Prompt Used

> What are some common communication challenges families may face?

### How AI Was Used

ChatGPT was used to provide general examples of family communication challenges.

The response helped the team think about possible problem areas, such as misunderstandings, difficulty expressing feelings, and disagreements between family members.

### Team Contribution

The team did the main work by:

- selecting family communication as the project focus
- deciding that the app should help both sides explain their perspective
- choosing empathy and shared understanding as the main goal
- developing the WASL idea independently

### Contribution to Version 1

This prompt supported early brainstorming only. The team created the actual app concept, purpose, and target users.

## Entry 2 — Choosing the Target Users

**Date:** 16 July 2026  
**AI Tool Used:** ChatGPT

### Prompt Used

> Who could benefit from a family communication app?

### How AI Was Used

ChatGPT suggested broad user groups such as parents, teenagers, siblings, and grandparents.

The response was used only as a basic reference while deciding who the app should be designed for.

### Team Contribution

The team:

- selected families as the main audience
- decided that teenagers and parents would be important users
- considered the needs of users with different ages and technology experience
- chose to keep the interface simple and mobile-friendly

### Contribution to Version 1

This prompt helped confirm the intended audience. The team made the final decision about the users and how the interface should look.

## Entry 3 — Planning the First Interface

**Date:** 20 July 2026  
**AI Tool Used:** ChatGPT

### Prompt Used

> What should a simple first screen for a family communication app include?

### How AI Was Used

ChatGPT gave general suggestions such as showing the app name, a short description, and a clear explanation of the app’s purpose.

The suggestions were used as inspiration only.

### Team Contribution

The team:

- created the WASL name and project identity
- chose the colors and visual direction
- decided what information should appear on the first page
- built the Version 1 interface
- reviewed and adjusted the layout based on the team’s own preferences

### Contribution to Version 1

This prompt supported the early interface planning. The final design, wording, structure, and implementation were completed by the team.

our team used AI only for limited support and did not use it to create the full application.

our team remained responsible for:

- choosing the project idea

- writing and organizing the project
